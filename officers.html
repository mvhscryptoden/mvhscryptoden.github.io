Officer Faq

Q: How do I become an officer?

A: Officers are elected once per year for the following school year. Students interested in becoming officers may declare their candidacy approximately one month before elections. During this period, multiple candidates may run for the same position. Club members then vote, and the student with the most votes is selected for each role.

Q: What does being an officer entail?

A: Officers help lead the club by organizing meetings, coordinating competitions and events, and promoting the club to prospective members. They are also responsible for completing required club paperwork, communicating with members, managing club operations, and ensuring the club runs smoothly throughout the school year.

Q: How much time does being an officer require?

A:The time commitment varies depending on the position and upcoming events. Officers are expected to attend meetings regularly and may spend additional time planning activities, communicating with members, completing paperwork, and preparing for competitions.

Q: Can officers participate in competitions?

A: Yes! Officers are encouraged to participate in competitions alongside other members. However, officers may also have additional responsibilities before, during, and after events to help coordinate club activities.

Q: What positions are available?

A: Officer positions typically include President, Vice President, Secretary, Treasurer, and other roles as needed. Each position has different responsibilities that contribute to the operation and growth of the club.
